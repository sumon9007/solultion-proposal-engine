## Exclusions

The following items are explicitly excluded from this proposal unless otherwise stated in the Scope of Work. If any of the following are required, they can be scoped and priced as a separate engagement.

### General Exclusions

1. Any work, system, or service not explicitly described in the Scope of Work section of this proposal.

2. End-user training, unless specifically listed as a deliverable. User adoption, change management communications, and training programme design are separate engagements.

3. Third-party vendor management or procurement. We will advise on vendor selection and licensing, but all contracts with third parties remain between the client and the vendor.

4. Remediation of pre-existing faults, technical debt, or security vulnerabilities identified during the engagement that fall outside the agreed scope.

5. Ongoing support and maintenance following project completion, unless a managed services agreement is in place.

6. Disaster recovery testing, unless explicitly included in the scope.

7. Hardware procurement, supply, installation, or decommissioning, unless explicitly stated in the scope.

8. Physical cabling, network infrastructure changes, or facilities work.

9. Business process redesign or organisational change management.

10. Legal, compliance, or regulatory advice. Where regulatory requirements affect technical decisions, we will flag these to the client for guidance from qualified legal or compliance professionals.

11. Data migration beyond the volumes and systems explicitly described in the scope. Additional data sources or volumes will require a Change Request.

12. Custom software development, scripting, or automation beyond what is explicitly described in the scope.

13. Penetration testing or formal security assessments, unless listed as a deliverable.

14. Any work required to bring the client's environment into a baseline-compliant state before the project can begin.
