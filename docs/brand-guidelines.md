# Brand Guidelines

Use these guidelines for all customer-facing proposal outputs, including Word documents, PowerPoint presentations, PDF exports, and HTML-rendered proposals.

## Brand Principles

- Clear over decorative
- Professional over promotional
- Consistent across channels
- Accessible in print and on screen

## Core Visual System

- Primary colour: `#0B5FFF`
- Secondary colour: `#0F172A`
- Accent colour: `#14B8A6`
- Neutral background: white or very light grey
- Heading font: Aptos Display, Calibri, or approved house heading font
- Body font: Aptos, Calibri, or approved house body font

## Typography

- Use one heading family and one body family only
- Keep heading hierarchy obvious: title, section heading, body
- Avoid dense text blocks longer than one page without subheadings
- Use sentence case for headings unless house style requires title case

## Word Document Guidance

- Use a branded cover page with proposal title, client name, date, version, and company identity
- Keep page margins generous for print readability
- Use heading styles rather than manual formatting so navigation and TOC work correctly
- Place contact details and confidentiality text in the footer, not in the body
- Use tables for pricing and timelines only when they improve clarity
- Keep legal notes and assumptions visually quieter than the executive narrative

## PowerPoint Presentation Guidance

- Limit each slide to one main message
- Use strong section divider slides for story flow
- Prefer diagrams, phased visuals, and concise bullets over paragraphs
- Keep slide titles insight-led, not generic
- Reserve the accent colour for emphasis, not for large text blocks
- Use proposal-consistent terminology and client naming throughout

Recommended deck sections:
- Title
- Executive summary
- Customer challenge
- Proposed solution
- Scope and approach
- Delivery timeline
- Commercial summary
- Why us
- Next steps

## PDF Export Guidance

- Design for print first: readable margins, page breaks, page numbering
- Ensure colour contrast remains readable when printed in grayscale
- Avoid relying on hyperlinks or hover states for core meaning
- Keep tables from splitting awkwardly across pages where possible
- Use final approved content only; no draft markers or review comments

## Content and Layout Rules

- No placeholder text
- No internal AI notes
- No conflicting terminology across Word, PowerPoint, and PDF outputs
- The same customer engagement should use the same title, client name format, and solution naming everywhere

## File-Type Expectations

- Word: editable working master for formal submission packaging when required
- PowerPoint: executive summary or proposal presentation companion, not a verbatim copy of the proposal
- PDF: frozen customer-facing distribution version derived from approved content
